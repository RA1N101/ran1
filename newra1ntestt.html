The reason I made RA1N is because

I hate restrictions.

The main reason I thought about creating RA1N in the first place was GoGuardian's strict restrictions. It's a pain when a tool designed to keep us safe ends up being a roadblock to what we love. In my case, it's programming. Just because we're kids doesn't mean we're clueless. We can think and spot bad stuff. The problem is these restrictions go overboard. I'm all for some rules, but there are just way too many. So, I started RA1N to find a balance. We get why safety is important, but there's got to be a middle ground. With RA1N, we aim to break free from excessive controls and build a place where we can learn and explore without feeling hemmed in. It's not about being rebellious; it's about having the freedom to grow responsibly in the digital world. That's what RA1N is all about.
